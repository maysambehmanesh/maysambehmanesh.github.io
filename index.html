Maysam Behmanesh

Postdoctoral Researcher at LIX, École Polytechnique/Inria

I'm a Postdoctoral Researcher in the GeoViC group at the LIX research laboratory of École Polytechnique Paris working with Prof. Maks Ovsjanikov. I graduated with Ph.D. in Computer Engineering (Artificial Intelligence) from University of Isfahan (UI) in February 2022. I worked for five years in machine learning topics under the supervision of Dr. Peyman Adibi. My current research interests are focused on developing geometric machine learning approaches on multimodal data including graphs and manifolds. From 2019 to 2020, I continued my research in GIPSA-Lab with Grenoble Institute of Technology, Grenoble, France, as a visiting scholar under the supervision of Prof. Jocelyn Chanussot. I completed my BSc. in Computer Engineering (software) and graduated in MSc. of Artificial Intelligence at University of Kerman (SBUK), Iran 2014, where I developed neural networks and neuro-fuzzy systems for time series prediction.

Research Interests

Multimodal Machine Learning

Developing multimodal models to fuse the encoded complementary information from multiple modalities and find a latent representation of data that models all modalities simultaneously. Representation learning of multimodal data has increasing applications in real-world problems such as medical and biological multimodal data processing, hyperspectral remote sensing, natural language processing, and etc.

2017 - Present

Geometric Multimodal Deep learning

Generalizing deep learning models to multimodal inherently or implicitly data defined on geometric structures such as manifolds and graphs. I am extremely interested in developing these models for biological and biomedical problems.

2019 - Present

Graph Convolutional Networks

Designing convolutional filters to learn the representation of graph-based data in spectral or spatial domains for application in the social and biological problems including node prediction, graph signal classification, link prediction, network diffusion, and network embedding.

2019 - Present

Imbalanced Data Analysis

Developing machine learning models for classification of imbalanced data. Imbalanced learning raises many critical questions in real-world applications such as, financial analysis, fraud detection, cheminformatics analysis , and biomedical data analysis.

2015 - Present

Time Series Analysis

Identifying of underlying system that dynamically produced the observed data, for many real-world applications such as, economic forecasting, sales forecasting, stock market analysis, weather forecasting, earthquake prediction, and etc.

2013 - 2016

Project 4

The project goal is developing a multi-scaled graph wavelet convolution network for analyzing the multimodal graph-based data, while it is not limited to either the multimodal domains with the same number of homogeneous data in each modality or any prior knowledge, including correspondences between modalities.

We generalized the spectral-based GCN method to graph-based multimodal data as a problem that has rarely been addressed.
We considerd a general scenario for multimodal problems with unpaired data in the heterogeneous modalities, which have no prior knowledge.
We introduced a new spectral-based GCN that parallelly applies graph wavelet transforms with different scaling parameters in each modality.
We developed a new efficient end-to-end network that simultaneously applies feature transform on each modality and explores permutations for representing the cross-modality correlations among various modalities.

  • Collaborators: Maysam Behmanesh, Peyman Adibi, Jocelyn Chanussot, and Saeed Ehsani
  • Date: December 2020-Present

Project 3

The project goals are:
1) Introducing new cross-modality retrieval framework for heterogeneous data without any specific prior knowledge.
2) Introducing a new method for multiclass classification of multimodal heterogeneous data without any specific prior knowledge.

We designed a new multimodal method, which is not limited to homogeneous modalities. It extracts the local descriptors of graph Laplacian of each modality using the spectral graph wavelet transform.
We developed a new loss function with two between-modality and within-modality regularizers, which is independent of direct correspondences between samples, while implicitly uses the semantic relationship between them.
We developed a new manifold regularized framework for multiclass classification of multimodal data, while takes advantage of the capabilities of mapping heterogeneous data in the kernel Hilbert spaces.

  • Collaborators: Maysam Behmanesh, Peyman Adibi, Jocelyn Chanussot, and Saeed Ehsani
  • Date: July 2019-October 2020

Project 2

The project goal is introducing a multimodal manifold model that extends spectral or diffusion geometry-aware data analysis to multiple modalities and achieves better results using only a few corresponding samples.

We extended manifold learning to multimodal scenarios in a semi-supervised setting by the propagation of the existing supervised information to their local neighborhoods.
We used a multimodal manifold learning framework by approximation of common Laplacian eigenbasis.
We introduced a local signal expansion method that employed the local alignment of tangent spaces to discover the local geometrical structure of matching and mismatching manifold parts in each modality.

  • Collaborators: Maysam Behmanesh, Peyman Adibi, Jocelyn Chanussot, Christian Jutten, and Saeed Ehsani
  • Date: March 2019-April 2020

Project 1

The project goal is introducing an approach that efficiently used fuzzy rough set theory in weighted least squares twin support vector machine for classification of imbalanced data.

We introduced a new under-sampling method based on the fuzzy rough set theory to reduce effectively the number of majority training points and make resistant to the outliers and imbalanced data.
We introduced a new strategy to overcome the bias phenomenon to majority class in the original TSVM.

  • Collaborators: Maysam Behmanesh, Peyman Adibi, and Hosein Karshenas
  • Date: March 2017-September 2017
